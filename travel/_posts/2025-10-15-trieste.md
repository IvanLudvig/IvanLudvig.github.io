Trieste is an unusual city in Italy.

After WW2, [the New Yorker of 1946](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/1946/11/30/letter-from-trieste){:target="_blank"} described the situation as follows, in an early Cold War fashion:
>  In a ravaged Adriatic borderland, Italians, Slavs, and Soviets struggle over a symbolic port that could open Europe to Russia. 

So it became [The Free Territory of Trieste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_Territory_of_Trieste){:target="_blank"}. After it dissolved in 1954, its Zone A went to Italy and Zone B was split between Slovenia and Croatia. 

The Grand Canal used to be a bit longer. Just a bit. I wish it reached Slovenia.
